USER MANUAL

User Manual

This guide helps you navigate the key features of the MediQnect.

Add Reminder Tab
  • Go to the "Reminders" tab to view your reminders.
  • Tap "Add Medicine" to enter the medicine name, details, and assign it to a compartment.
  • When choosing a compartment, you'll see available (vacant) and unavailable (occupied) compartments.
  • If a compartment is occupied, the system will prevent you from selecting it and show a message like "Occupied." If it's vacant, you can assign your medicine to it successfully.
  • After saving the medicine, tap "Add Reminder" to set when the medicine should be taken.
  • Tap "Submit" to save the reminder. It will now appear in the Medication Timeline and calendar.
  • Tap "Show Medicines" to view all added medicines.
  • While in the Show Medicines view, you can edit or delete any medicine.
  • Tap "Show Schedules" to view all the reminders and their scheduled times.
  • While in the Show Schedules view, you can delete any schedule if needed.
 History Tab
  • Tap the "History" tab to view medication logs.
  • History includes:
    • Complete – Taken from the dispenser
    • Missed – Not taken
    • Late – Taken outside the scheduled time
  • Tap a missed medication to update its status.
  • Choose from the following:
    • Correct Time – Moves to Complete
    • Specific Time – Moves to Late
 Profile Menu
  • Tap the profile icon in the top-left corner.
  • The menu includes:
    • Change Password
    • Switch Account
    • Add Account
    • Delete Account
    • Clean Compartment

      Once a liquid medication is finished or has expired, leftover residue may remain inside the compartment. To ensure cleanliness and avoid contamination, users must clean the compartment.

      Tap "Clean Compartment" and choose which compartment to clean (B1, B2, or B3). After selecting, you will be asked to add water to rinse the compartment. If residue is still visible after the first cleaning, simply repeat the process.

    • Logout
 Dispenser
  • To open the dispenser for refilling or maintenance, use the main switch button.
    This physical switch is typically located at the top or front of the dispenser. Once pressed, it unlocks the lid and opens the entire unit.
  • The dispenser contains six compartments: three for pills and three for liquids.
  • During scheduled times, an alarm will sound and an LED light will illuminate on the correct compartment.
  • Press the dispense button to release the medication:
    • Liquid medicines are released automatically through the nozzle.
    • Pills are dispensed based on the scheduled dosage.
  • A built-in water container allows users to drink water after taking medicine.
  • The dispenser is equipped with a UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply), ensuring it continues to function even during power outages.
  • Expired medicines are automatically released into four drawers:
    • 1 drawer for liquid medicines
    • 3 drawers for different types of pills
  • After dispensing, the system sends a notification to your connected device to confirm intake.

This manual is designed to help you use the app effectively and manage your medication intake.
